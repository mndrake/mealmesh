# Low-Carb T2D Meal Plan — BEGINNER EDITION
### Every recipe as numbered steps · with heat levels, times & doneness temps

If you've never really cooked, start here. This version assumes **nothing**. Read the four reference sections first (Equipment, Techniques, Doneness, Base Recipes), then cook from the month you're on. The shopping lists and net carbs are unchanged from the master plan — this just adds the missing "how."

**The golden rule for a new cook:** buy a cheap **instant-read thermometer** (~$12). It removes all guesswork and keeps you safe. When a step gives a temperature, that's your finish line — trust the number, not the clock.

> General guidance, not medical advice. Confirm carb targets and medication timing with your care team.

# 2 · TECHNIQUE GLOSSARY
*Plain-language definitions. Recipes reference these by name.*

- **Preheat** — let the oven (or pan) fully reach temperature *before* food goes in. Ovens take ~10 min.
- **Sear** — hot pan + a little oil, lay the food down, and **don't move it** for a few minutes so a brown crust forms. Browning = flavor.
- **Sauté** — cook in a little oil/butter over medium-high, **stirring often**, until softened or browned.
- **Simmer** — gentle, small bubbles (medium-low heat). Not a violent boil.
- **Boil** — big rolling bubbles, high heat.
- **Wilt** — cook leafy greens 1–2 min just until they collapse and shrink.
- **Render** — cook fatty meat (sausage, chorizo, bacon) so the fat melts out and it browns.
- **Dredge** — coat food in flour (here, almond flour) before frying so it crisps.
- **Reduce** — let a liquid simmer so water evaporates and it thickens.
- **Deglaze** — pour liquid into a hot pan and scrape up the browned bits stuck to the bottom (that's flavor).
- **Rest** (meat) — after cooking, let it sit 3–5 min before slicing so the juices stay in instead of running out.
- **Against the grain** — meat has visible lines running one direction. Slice **across** them, not along them. This is what makes a slice tender vs. chewy.

# 3 · DONENESS TEMPERATURE CHART
*Stick the thermometer into the thickest part. This is also your food-safety guarantee.*

| Food | Pull it at | Notes |
|------|-----------|-------|
| Chicken (any cut) | **165°F** | No pink, juices run clear |
| Ground turkey | **165°F** | — |
| Ground beef / pork | **160°F** | — |
| Pork chops / tenderloin (whole) | **145°F** | Then rest 3 min |
| Steak | **130–135°F** med-rare · **140–145°F** medium | Then rest 5 min |
| **Shrimp** | *no thermometer* | Pink, opaque, curled into a loose **C**. A tight **O** = overcooked. ~2–3 min/side |
| Egg bites / quiche | **~160°F** | Centers set, no liquid jiggle |

# 4 · BASE RECIPES & PREP SKILLS
*Things the recipes reuse. Learn these once.*

**Dice an onion / bell pepper** — Cut in half through the root. For pepper, pull out the core and seeds. Lay flat-side down, slice into strips, then crosswise into squares.

**Thaw & dry shrimp (do this every time)** — Put frozen shrimp in a bowl of cold water 15 min. Drain and **pat very dry** with paper towels. Wet shrimp steams and turns rubbery; dry shrimp sears.

**Perfect cauliflower mash (not watery)** — Steam or boil florets 10 min until fork-tender. Drain in a colander and **let sit 5 min, pressing out water** with a spoon. *Then* blend with butter, cream, and salt. Skipping the drain = soup.

**Low-carb gravy that actually thickens** — Broth and cream won't thicken on their own like flour gravy. After adding the liquid, sprinkle in **⅛–¼ tsp xanthan gum** while whisking constantly; simmer 2 min. No xanthan? Just simmer longer to reduce — it'll be thinner.

**Grease the muffin tin well** — Oil every cup or use silicone liners. Egg bites cement themselves to bare metal.

**Soak wooden skewers** — 30 min in water before grilling, or they char and snap.

**Tzatziki** (Month 3) — 1 cup Greek yogurt + ½ cucumber grated and squeezed dry + 1 grated garlic clove + 1 tbsp lemon + chopped dill + salt. Stir.

**Chicken tikka marinade** (Month 4) — ½ cup Greek yogurt + 1 tbsp garam masala + 1 tsp cumin + 1 tsp paprika + grated garlic & ginger + 1 tbsp lemon + salt. Coat chicken, marinate 30 min minimum (overnight is better — do it Sunday).

**Hard-boil eggs** — Cover eggs with water, bring to a boil, turn off heat, cover, wait 12 min. Move to ice water, then peel.

**Oven bacon** — Lay strips on a foil-lined sheet pan, bake at 400°F for 15 min until crisp.

# 5 · PHOTO-STYLE TECHNIQUE WALKTHROUGHS
*This is a written guide, so each tricky technique below is described as if you were flipping through a photo sequence — what it looks like, sounds like, and smells like at each stage, plus the exact moment to act. Read the one you need right before you cook it.*

### A · A Proper Sear (steak, chops, chicken)
- **Stage 1 — Setup:** The meat is patted bone-dry, surface matte (not glistening). The empty pan sits on high ~2 min — flick in a water drop and it dances and vanishes instantly. Add a thin film of oil; it shimmers and *just* begins to wisp smoke.
- **Stage 2 — The lay-down:** Place the meat down *away from you*. It sizzles **loudly and immediately** — a steady, aggressive hiss. Silent = pan too cool; violent spitting = too hot. Now **don't touch it.**
- **Stage 3 — The crust (2–3 min):** The edge color creeps from pink/red up to grey-brown. When it's ready, the meat **releases on its own** — if it grips the pan when you nudge, give it another 30 sec. A finished face is deep golden-brown to mahogany, dry-looking, with darker freckles.
- **Stage 4 — Flip & finish:** Second side the same. Juices bead on the cooked surface; center hits your target temp. Burnished, not grey and steamed.

### B · Crisp-Tender Vegetables (broccoli, green beans, peppers)
- **Raw:** bright, rigid, squeaky.
- **Midway (3–4 min):** color deepens to vivid green, brown blistered spots appear at the edges, pieces bend slightly without flopping.
- **Done:** a fork slides in with light resistance — tender outside, faint bite in the center. Drab and floppy = gone too far.

### C · When Shrimp Is Done (the C vs the O)
- **Raw:** grey, see-through, lying nearly straight or in a loose hook.
- **Cooking:** edges turn pink-white, the opaque color creeping inward.
- **DONE — pull it now:** bright pink/coral outside, white and opaque all the way through, curled into a loose **C** (like a comma).
- **Overcooked:** curled into a tight **O** ring, shrunken and rubbery. The window between C and O is about **30 seconds** — hover over the pan.

### D · Dicing an Onion
- Halve it through the root; peel. Lay a flat side down.
- Make horizontal then vertical cuts pointing *toward* the root (not through it), then slice crosswise — even little squares fall away.
- **Picture it:** pieces about the size of a pencil eraser. Keep fingertips curled under so your knuckles guide the blade.

### E · Coring a Bell Pepper
- Stand it on end. Slice the four "walls" off around the center, turning it like a box. The white core and seed cluster stay behind in one piece — toss it.
- Lay the walls flat, slice into strips, then crosswise into squares.

### F · Cauliflower Mash — The Drain That Makes or Breaks It
- **After boiling:** florets are pale and fall apart when pressed.
- **The critical moment:** tip them into a colander and press with a spoon — **cloudy water streams out.** Keep pressing until it stops running and the surface looks dry and crumbly, not glossy.
- **Blended right:** smooth and thick enough to hold a soft peak, like mashed potato. If it slumps into a puddle, it needed more draining.

### G · Low-Carb Gravy Thickening (xanthan gum)
- **Before:** thin, watery broth that sloshes freely and coats nothing.
- **Adding it:** sprinkle the xanthan in a *thin dusting* while whisking fast. Dumping it in one spot makes slimy clumps.
- **After 1–2 min:** the liquid pulls together, takes on a slight sheen and body, and a spoon dragged across the pan bottom leaves a brief **path** before it closes back up. It now coats the back of a spoon.

### H · Dredging Chicken (egg → almond flour)
- **After the egg dip:** the cutlet is fully wet, glossy, dripping a little.
- **After the almond flour:** press both sides in — the surface turns matte, sandy, and pale, with **no wet patches** peeking through.
- **In the pan:** the coating sets into a golden, craggy crust.

### I · Slicing Against the Grain
- Look closely at the cooked meat: you'll see faint parallel fibers running one direction, like wood grain.
- Turn your knife **perpendicular (90°)** to those lines and slice across them. Each piece shows short fiber ends.
- **Wrong way (along the grain):** long stringy fibers = chewy bites.

### J · Wilting Spinach
- **Raw:** a huge mounded pile overflowing the pan — it looks like far too much.
- **60–90 sec, tossing:** it collapses dramatically to a fraction of the size, glossy and dark green, soft and silky. Pull it the instant it's all dark — past that it weeps water and turns mushy.

### K · Egg Bites — The Jiggle Test
- **Not ready:** the center looks wet and shiny and ripples like liquid when you nudge the pan.
- **Done:** the tops are puffed and matte, spring back to a light touch, and the center has at most a faint wobble (firm jello, not liquid). They deflate slightly as they cool — that's normal.

### L · Rendering Sausage / Chorizo
- **Start:** clumped, raw, pale or red.
- **Midway:** fat pools and sizzles, the meat browns at the edges and breaks into crumbles.
- **Done:** deep-browned bits, glistening rendered fat, zero raw red remaining.

### M · Zucchini Ribbons with a Peeler
- Run a vegetable peeler down the length with firm, even pressure — long flat ribbons peel off, pale with a green edge stripe. Rotate the zucchini as you go; stop at the soft seedy core.
- **In the pan:** just 2 min until floppy and glossy. They go limp and release water fast, so keep it brief.

### N · Rolling Meatballs & Kofta
- The mix should be **tacky and hold together** when pinched — not soupy. Too wet? A spoon of almond flour or parmesan firms it up.
- **Meatballs:** scoop a golf-ball amount, roll between your palms into a smooth sphere.
- **Kofta:** shape into a 3–4 inch log around two fingers, slightly flattened, like a small sausage.

# 🍳 MONTH 1 — Classics

## MENU A (Weeks 1 & 3)

### ☀️ Breakfast — Sausage & Spinach Egg Bites
**Equipment:** muffin tin (greased), skillet, bowl, whisk · **Net carbs:** ~2g
1. Preheat oven to **400°F**. Grease all 12 muffin cups well, or use liners.
2. In a skillet over medium-high, cook **8 oz sausage**, breaking it up, until no pink remains (~6 min, **160°F**). Drain; cool a few minutes.
3. In a bowl, whisk **10 eggs + ¼ cup cream** + a pinch of salt and pepper.
4. Stir in the sausage, **5 oz chopped spinach**, and **1 cup shredded cheddar**.
5. Pour evenly into the cups (about ¾ full).
6. Bake **18–20 min** until centers are set with no liquid jiggle (**~160°F**). Cool 5 min, lift out.
7. **Store:** fridge 4 days; freeze the rest. Reheat 30–45 sec in the microwave. Eat 3 per person.

### 🥗 Lunch — Chicken Cobb Jar
**Equipment:** mason jars · **Net carbs:** ~7g
*Cook the chicken first (Sunday):* Preheat oven **400°F**. Rub chicken breast with oil, salt, pepper. Roast on a sheet pan **22–25 min** until **165°F**. Rest 5 min, slice.
**Build each jar:**
1. Pour **2 tbsp dressing** into the bottom.
2. Add **4 oz sliced chicken**, **1 chopped hard-boiled egg**, **1 crumbled bacon strip**, **2 tbsp blue cheese**, **¼ cup halved tomatoes**.
3. Pack **romaine** on top (keeps everything dry). Seal; fridge up to 4 days.
4. Morning-of: add **½ diced avocado**. At lunch, shake to coat.

### 🍽️ Dinners

**Monday — Sheet-Pan Chicken & Broccoli** · **Net carbs:** ~7g
1. Preheat oven to **425°F**.
2. Cut **1.5 lb broccoli** into florets. Toss with **2 tbsp olive oil**, **½ tsp garlic powder**, salt. Spread on a sheet pan in one layer.
3. If using raw chicken thighs, rub with oil/salt and nestle them in.
4. Roast **20–25 min** until broccoli edges brown and chicken reads **165°F**. *(Pre-cooked chicken? Add it for just the last 8 min to reheat.)*

**Tuesday — Beef & Pepper Stir-Fry** · **Net carbs:** ~10g
1. Slice **1 lb steak** thinly **against the grain**; pat dry. Slice **2 bell peppers + ½ onion**.
2. Heat **1 tbsp oil** in a skillet over **high** until it shimmers.
3. Add beef in one layer; don't stir for 1 min, then sear 2–3 min until browned. Remove.
4. Add peppers + onion; stir-fry 4–5 min until crisp-tender.
5. Return beef; add **2 tbsp soy sauce + 1 minced garlic clove**; toss 1 min.

**Wednesday — Shrimp Scampi over Zoodles** · **Net carbs:** ~8g
1. **Thaw & dry 1 lb shrimp.** Make zoodles from **3 zucchini** (spiralizer, or peeler ribbons).
2. Melt **3 tbsp butter** in a skillet over medium. Add **3 minced garlic cloves**; cook 30 sec (don't brown).
3. Add shrimp in one layer; cook **2 min/side** until pink and curled into a **C**. Remove.
4. Add zoodles; toss 2 min just to warm (longer = watery).
5. Return shrimp; finish with a squeeze of lemon + parsley.

**Thursday — Pork Chops & Cauliflower Mash** · **Net carbs:** ~9g
1. Make **cauliflower mash** from 1 head (see Base Recipes — drain well!).
2. Pat **2 pork chops** dry; season both sides.
3. Heat **1 tbsp oil** over medium-high. Sear chops **4–5 min/side** until **145°F**.
4. **Rest 3 min**, then serve over the mash.

**Friday — Burger Bowls** · **Net carbs:** ~6g
1. Form **1 lb ground beef** into 2 patties; season.
2. Heat a skillet over medium-high. Sear patties **3–4 min/side** until **160°F**.
3. Make a bed of chopped romaine in 2 bowls.
4. Top with the patty, **¼ cup cheddar** each (melts on the hot patty), tomatoes, and sugar-free ketchup/mustard.

## MENU B (Weeks 2 & 4)

### ☀️ Breakfast — Vanilla Chia Pudding + Berries
**No cooking** · **Net carbs:** ~8g
1. In a bowl, whisk **6 tbsp chia seeds, 1.5 cups almond milk, ½ tsp vanilla**, sweetener to taste.
2. Wait 5 min, whisk again to break up clumps.
3. Divide into 5 jars; chill **overnight** (it sets to pudding).
4. Each morning, top one jar with **¼ cup berries**. *(Savory swap: ham & cheese egg muffins — use the egg-bite method.)*

### 🥗 Lunch — Shrimp & Avocado Box · **Net carbs:** ~7g
*Sunday:* thaw & dry **1 lb shrimp**; sauté in 1 tbsp oil over medium-high **2 min/side** until pink and curled; chill.
**Build each box:** 5 oz shrimp, romaine, ¼ cup tomatoes, 2 tbsp dressing on the side, a lime wedge. Add **½ avocado** morning-of.

### 🍽️ Dinners

**Monday — Chicken Fajita Bowls** · **Net carbs:** ~12g
1. *(If chicken isn't pre-cooked)* Season **1 lb chicken** with fajita seasoning; sear over medium-high **5–6 min/side** until **165°F**; slice.
2. Slice **2 peppers + ½ onion**; sauté in 1 tbsp oil over medium-high 6 min until soft and charred at the edges.
3. In another pan, sauté **2 bags cauliflower rice** in 1 tbsp oil 5 min.
4. Build bowls: cauli rice, chicken, peppers/onion, top with cheese.

**Tuesday — Meatballs & Marinara over Zoodles** · **Net carbs:** ~11g
1. Preheat oven **400°F**.
2. Mix **1.25 lb ground meat + 1 egg + ¼ cup mozzarella** + salt/pepper. Roll ~16 balls.
3. Bake on a sheet pan **18 min** until **160°F**.
4. Warm marinara in a pot; add meatballs; simmer 5 min.
5. Make zoodles from **3 zucchini**; toss in a hot dry pan 2 min. Serve sauce + meatballs over zoodles.

**Wednesday — Garlic Butter Shrimp & Green Beans** · **Net carbs:** ~7g
1. **Thaw & dry 1 lb shrimp.** Trim **1.5 lb green beans.**
2. Sauté beans in **3 tbsp butter** over medium-high 6 min until crisp-tender.
3. Push beans aside; add shrimp + **3 minced garlic cloves**; cook **2 min/side** until pink and curled.

**Thursday — Steak & Roasted Asparagus** · **Net carbs:** ~5g
1. Preheat oven **425°F**. Toss **1.5 lb asparagus** with oil/salt; roast 12 min.
2. Pat **1 lb steak** dry; season. Heat a skillet over **high**; sear **3–4 min/side** (**130–135°F** for medium-rare).
3. **Rest 5 min**, then slice **against the grain**.

**Friday — Sausage & Cabbage Skillet** · **Net carbs:** ~9g
1. Slice **14 oz smoked sausage** into coins. Brown in a skillet over medium-high 5 min (it's already cooked — just browning).
2. Add **½ shredded cabbage + ½ sliced onion + 2 tbsp butter**.
3. Sauté 10 min until the cabbage is tender.

# 🍂 MONTH 2 — Comfort & Skillet

## MENU A (Weeks 1 & 3)

### ☀️ Breakfast — Western Omelet Egg Muffins
**Equipment:** muffin tin (greased), skillet · **Net carbs:** ~3g
1. Preheat oven to **400°F**. Grease 12 muffin cups or use liners.
2. Sauté **½ diced onion + 1 diced pepper** in a little oil over medium-high 4 min until soft. Cool a few minutes.
3. Whisk **10 eggs + ¼ cup cream** + salt/pepper. Stir in the veg, **6 oz diced ham**, **1 cup cheddar**.
4. Fill the cups ¾ full. Bake **18–20 min** until set (**~160°F**). Cool 5 min, lift out. Eat 3 per person.

### 🥗 Lunch — Chef Salad Jar · **Net carbs:** ~6g
**Build each jar:** 2 tbsp ranch on the bottom → **1.5 oz ham, 1.5 oz turkey, 1 chopped hard-boiled egg, 1 oz cheddar, ¼ cup tomatoes** → romaine packed on top. Shake at lunch.

### 🍽️ Dinners

**Monday — Salisbury Steak + Mushroom Gravy & Green Beans** · **Net carbs:** ~9g
1. Form **1 lb ground beef** into 2 oval patties; season.
2. Heat **1 tbsp oil** in a skillet over medium-high. Sear patties **3–4 min/side** until **160°F**. Remove.
3. In the same pan, sauté **8 oz sliced mushrooms** 5 min until browned.
4. Add **¾ cup beef broth**; scrape up the brown bits (**deglaze**). To thicken: whisk in **⅛ tsp xanthan gum** (or simmer 8 min to reduce). Simmer 2 min.
5. Return patties to warm. Steam **1 lb green beans** 5 min alongside.

**Tuesday — Creamy Tuscan Chicken over Cauliflower Mash** · **Net carbs:** ~10g
1. Make **cauliflower mash** (1 head — drain well).
2. Season **1.5 lb chicken**; sear over medium-high **5–6 min/side** until **165°F**. Remove; slice.
3. In the same pan: **½ cup cream + ¼ cup chopped sun-dried tomatoes + 3 oz spinach**; simmer 3 min until spinach wilts.
4. Return chicken; serve over the mash.

**Wednesday — Cajun Shrimp & Sausage Skillet** · **Net carbs:** ~8g
1. **Thaw & dry 1 lb shrimp.** Slice **12 oz sausage.**
2. Brown sausage + **1 sliced pepper** over medium-high 6 min.
3. Add shrimp + **1 tbsp Cajun seasoning**; cook **2–3 min/side** until pink and curled.

**Thursday — Smothered Pork Chops & Mushrooms** · **Net carbs:** ~6g
1. Pat **2 chops** dry; season. Sear over medium-high **4–5 min/side** until **145°F**. Remove; rest.
2. Sauté **8 oz mushrooms + 1 garlic clove** 5 min. Add **¼ cup cream**; simmer 2 min (pinch of xanthan gum to thicken).
3. Return chops to coat.

**Friday — Philly Cheesesteak Bowls** · **Net carbs:** ~9g
1. Slice **1 pepper + ½ onion**; sauté in 1 tbsp oil over medium-high 6 min. Remove.
2. Add **1 lb shaved steak** to the hot pan; cook 3–4 min, breaking it apart, until no longer pink.
3. Return the veg, lay **4 oz provolone** on top, cover 1 min to melt.

## ✅ A Beginner's First-Week Game Plan
- **Start with Month 1, Menu A.** It's the most forgiving rotation.
- **Cook one new thing at a time.** Don't run the oven and three pans on Night 1 — build up to the Sunday multitask.
- **The thermometer is your training wheels.** Use it on every piece of meat until the look and feel become familiar.
- **Shrimp is the easiest protein to overcook** — pull it the second it curls into a C.
- **When a sauce won't thicken,** that's normal for low-carb — reduce it longer or use the xanthan gum trick.
